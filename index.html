Dual thesis student

I study feedback in astrophysical and built systems. In galaxies, I trace how active nuclei change gas and light. In space design, I focus on routines that make orbit livable. These lines of work share a method: start from the real behaviors of the system and write plainly about what it takes to keep it going.

Astrophysics thesis

Mid-infrared forbidden lines and ionizing spectra in low-metallicity LyC emitters

Advisor: Dr. Anne Jaskot

“Ancient photons cross large distances to reach us. What we see depends on how freely light can move through gas.”

I use JWST spectroscopy of nearby low-metallicity galaxies to study line ratios that carry information about the hardness of ionizing radiation. The goal is to connect spectra to escape of ionizing light.

WGSS thesis

Feminist infrastructures of breath, waste, and feeling in orbital design

Advisor: Dr. Ezra Feldman

“Airflow is not background. It is made by fans, filters, and people who tune them.”

I write about maintenance and care in closed environments. I look at how procedures, interfaces, and habits keep bodies comfortable and safe in orbit.

Current research

I work with galaxy spectra, data systems for modular space structures, and public-facing writing about astronomy and place.

Hidden black holes in DESI

I analyze DESI spectra and use [O III] λ5008 and [Ne V] λ3426 to find active nuclei, including obscured cases. I use model fits and tests to separate signal from artifacts and then connect line properties to star-forming histories.

AAS 2025 Preprint

Self-assembling space structures

At Aurelia Institute I wrote Python tools to process many telemetry streams from TESSERAE modules. The code aligns sensors, finds docking events, and summarizes system behavior for design decisions.

KNAC 2025 Project

Radio astronomy curriculum

With a small grant I set up a radio astronomy program, trained students, and connected a university to NRAO mentors. The aim was simple: build local capacity so students can keep going after a workshop ends.

AAS abstract

Telescope siting and community

I study how sites are chosen and what that means for nearby communities. I read archives, compare cases, and write about process and consent.

The journey so far

Honduran rainforest roots

I grew up in a rural area near Blanca Jeannette Kawas National Park in San Alejo, Tela. Dark skies were close. On Fridays we would sit outside and name the stars together.

Physics Olympiad and the pandemic

Three bronze medals for Honduras. I kept going through COVID-19 and two hurricanes while applying to college abroad.

Williams College

PWIs were not built with students like me in mind. I started the Wavelength Club to make a place for minoritized physics students.

Princeton and NRAO

I worked on AGN in DESI spectra and studied how big facilities choose sites. I try to keep technical work and public impact in the same frame.
